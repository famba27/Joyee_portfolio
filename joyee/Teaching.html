Teaching

KNH school of nursing has been in operation for the last 36 years. It trains Nurses at higher diploma level.

These are nurses who have already had a training in nursing at either a diploma or bachelorâ€™s level and who wish to specialize in either Critical care, Nephrology, Neonatal, Oncology, Perioperative or trauma and Emergency Nursing programs.
All these courses are regulated by the Nursing Council of Kenya (NCK). I was assigned lessons in Fundamentals of Nursing practice, in all the six courses,
and facilitated quite a number of specific topics in Nephrology nursing; being my area of specialty.
Some topics were taught virtually while some were taught physically.
I was also involved in setting, moderating, administering, marking and dispatching examination results. I was able to facilitate learning using various teaching,
and instructional strategies that included modified interactive lectures, small group discussions, individual student mentoring sessions.
Some of the instructional media included use laptop and LCD projectors, white board , marker pens, flip charts, and speakers.
These helped make the lessons quite interactive and interesting.
